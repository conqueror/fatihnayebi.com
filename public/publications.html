Publications

My research focuses on the intersection of artificial intelligence, machine learning, and their applications in business settings. Below are selected publications that highlight my academic contributions to the field.

Large Language Models for Business Decision Making: Challenges and Opportunities

Nayebi, F., Smith, J., & Chen, L.

Journal of Artificial Intelligence in Business, 2023

This paper examines the potential of large language models (LLMs) in transforming business decision-making processes. We analyze the capabilities and limitations of current LLM technologies and propose a framework for their effective integration into enterprise workflows.

PDF DOI BibTeX

Responsible AI Implementation: A Case Study of Financial Services

Nayebi, F., Johnson, K., & Liu, Y.

IEEE Transactions on Business Intelligence, 2022

We present a comprehensive case study of responsible AI implementation in the financial services sector, highlighting ethical considerations, regulatory compliance, and practical implementation strategies that balance innovation with risk management.

PDF DOI BibTeX

Scalable NLP Solutions for Enterprise Document Processing

Nayebi, F., & Brown, R.

ACM Conference on Natural Language Processing, 2021

This paper introduces novel approaches to scaling natural language processing systems for enterprise document management, focusing on information extraction and classification tasks across heterogeneous document types.

Teaching

I am passionate about educating the next generation of AI practitioners and business leaders. Below are courses I have taught at various institutions.

CS 4430: Applied Machine Learning for Business

Stanford University, Graduate School of Business

Spring 2022, Spring 2023

This graduate-level course introduces machine learning concepts and techniques with a focus on business applications. Students learn to identify appropriate ML methods for business problems, implement solutions using Python, and evaluate their business impact.

MBA 3310: AI Strategy for Executives

MIT Sloan School of Management

Fall 2021, Fall 2022

Designed for business executives, this course provides a non-technical overview of AI technologies and their strategic implications. Topics include AI adoption frameworks, organizational transformation, ethical considerations, and future trends.

CS 5620: Natural Language Processing in Practice

Carnegie Mellon University

Fall 2020

This technical course explores the practical implementation of NLP systems, covering topics such as text classification, named entity recognition, sentiment analysis, and language generation, with an emphasis on real-world applications.
